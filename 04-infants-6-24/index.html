Infants (6–24 Months)

Appropriate complementary feeding supports growth, motor skills, and diverse taste development.

Subtopics

Start at 6 Months

  • Introduce mashed/soft foods 2–3 times/day at first.
  • Increase frequency as child grows.

Food Variety

  • Include cereals, pulses, vegetables, fruits, milk, oils.
  • Continue breastfeeding up to 2 years & beyond.

Texture Progression

  • Mashed → soft lumps → finger foods by 9–12 months.
  • Avoid prolonged purees beyond 12 months.

Early Obesity Risk Behaviours

BehaviourWhy it matters
Processed snacks (biscuits, chips)Promote salty/sweet taste preference, excess calories.
Delayed texturesIncreases picky eating; oral–motor delays.
Sugary 'health drinks'Empty calories with little nutrition.

Key Messages

Start solids at 6 months.Family foods, not packets.Advance textures on time.
